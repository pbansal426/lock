GUYS! Please lock in for the tests

Short-term focus today leads to long-term gains tomorrow. Here's why locking in matters — and how to do it well.

Why studying is important

  • Understand, don't memorize: Studying builds understanding that helps you apply knowledge in new situations — tests, projects, and real life.
  • Reduce stress: Regular preparation prevents last-minute cramming and lowers anxiety before the exam.
  • Better retention: Spaced practice helps move information from short-term to long-term memory.
  • Opportunities: Good test performance opens doors — classes, scholarships, and confidence for future challenges.

How to lock in (practical tips)

Plan small sessions

Study 25–45 minute focused blocks with short breaks (Pomodoro). It's easier to stay consistent than to do one marathon session.

Active practice

Do problems, explain concepts aloud, quiz yourself — active recall beats passive review.

Prioritize weak spots

Spend slightly more time on topics you struggle with instead of repeatedly re-reading what you already know.

Mock tests

Simulate test conditions: time yourself, eliminate distractions, and practice with old exams if available.
